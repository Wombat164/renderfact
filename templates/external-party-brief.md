# Briefing: [External organisation name]

## Purpose of this meeting

State in one line why the two organisations are talking and what a good outcome looks
like from this session, not the full history of the relationship.

[One-line purpose] so that [the outcome we want from this conversation].

## Context they need

Give the external reader only the background their decision depends on: what changed,
what stayed the same, and what their organisation specifically needs to know to engage
usefully. Skip context that only makes sense inside your own organisation.

[Two to three sentences: the situation as the external party needs to understand it.]

::: {.block clearance="internal" releasable="team"}
Internal-only background (do not carry into the shared copy): [the real pressure behind
this outreach, such as an internal deadline, a lapsing arrangement, or a prior attempt
that stalled]. It explains our urgency but should not become their leverage.
:::

## What is shareable

Set the boundary explicitly rather than leaving the external reader to guess. A short

| [General scope and timeline]   | Yes |     |     |

| [Names of other parties involved] |  |     | Yes |

## Our ask

Name exactly what you want from this organisation, phrased as a request they can accept,
decline, or counter, not a wish.

[We ask that [organisation] provide / confirm / commit to: specific ask.]

## What we offer

Match the ask with what your organisation brings, so the exchange reads as a trade, not
a demand.

[In return, we offer: specific offer, such as access, volume, timeline certainty, or a
reference relationship.]

::: {.block clearance="confidential" releasable="team"}
Negotiating room (in confidence): the furthest we can move on [the ask above] without a
further internal approval is [limit or figure]. Do not disclose this limit; let the
external party make the first move on that point.
:::

## Boundaries and constraints

State plainly what is out of scope for this conversation and any constraint the external
party must respect (confidentiality, timing, who else may be told).

[Boundary one: what this conversation does not cover.]
[Boundary two: any confidentiality or timing constraint the other party must honour.]
