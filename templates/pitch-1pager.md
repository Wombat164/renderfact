# Pitch: [Idea name in five words or fewer]

## The ask

Lead with exactly what you want the reader to do, in one line. Everything below exists
to make this line easy to say yes to.

[Approve / fund / introduce me to: the single ask], decision needed by [date].

## The problem

State what is broken or missing today, for whom, and what it currently costs them in
time, money, or risk. One paragraph, not a survey of the whole domain.

[Two to three sentences: who is affected, what fails today, and what that costs them.]

## The proposed answer

Describe the fix at the level a busy reader can picture, not the implementation detail.

[Two to three sentences: what we would build, buy, or change, and why this approach over
the obvious alternative.]

## The value

Give the reader a reason to care that survives a single skim; a short table beats a
paragraph of adjectives here.

| Benefit                     | Who feels it          | Evidence                     |
|-------------------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------------|
| [Benefit one]                 | [Affected group]          | [Data point or source]         |
| [Benefit two]                 | [Affected group]          | [Data point or source]         |

::: {.block clearance="internal" releasable="team"}
Internal-only comparison: we also considered [alternative approach] and set it aside
because [reason], which is worth knowing before this pitch reaches anyone outside the
immediate team.
:::

## Cost and effort

One line: what this costs and how long it takes, stated as a band, not a false-precision
number this early.

[Cost band] over [duration], requiring [the resource this mainly needs: people, budget,
or time from a specific team].

::: {.block clearance="confidential" releasable="team"}
Working estimate detail (in confidence, not yet a committed figure): the cost band above
is built from [rough breakdown, such as licensing plus one part-time role for N months].
Treat it as directional until a proper estimate is commissioned.
:::

## Risk if we do not act

One sentence on what happens if this idea is shelved, so inaction reads as a choice with
a cost, not a free option.

[One sentence: the cost of doing nothing.]

::: {.block softspot="true"}
Honest caveat: [the one assumption this pitch leans on hardest, shared so the reader can
weigh it rather than discover it later].
:::
