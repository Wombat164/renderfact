# Purchase Request: [Item or service name]

## Need statement

State what business need this purchase closes and what happens if it stays unmet. One
paragraph; the requirements section carries the detail, not this one.

[Two to three sentences: the need, who has it, and the cost of leaving it unmet.]

## Background

Give only the context a reviewer needs to judge whether this request is well-formed:
what exists today, if anything, and why it no longer meets the need above.

[Two to three sentences: current state and why a purchase, rather than an internal fix,
is the right response.]

## Cost estimate

Give the reviewer a working figure and its basis; precision theatre this early helps no
one, so state a band unless a firm quote already exists.

| Cost element              | Estimate         | Basis                          |
|-------------------------------|--------------------|------------------------------------|
| [One-time cost, e.g. setup]   | [Cost band]         | [Quote, benchmark, or prior spend] |
| [Recurring cost, e.g. run]    | [Cost band per period] | [Quote, benchmark, or prior spend] |
| Total (indicative)            | [Cost band]         |                                     |

::: {.block clearance="confidential" releasable="team"}
Internal ceiling (in confidence, never for a market-facing copy): the maximum this
request is authorised to spend is [currency amount], approved by [budget holder]. Any
offer above this figure returns to the budget holder before proceeding, not to the
requester.
:::

::: {.block variant="abstract"}

follows once market responses are in hand.
:::
